# Computational Methods for Stochastic Differential Equations and Machine Learning

This repository contains theoretical research, computational implementations, and analytical reports developed for the SF2525 Computational Methods for Stochastic Differential Equations and Machine Learning course as part of the MSc in Applied and Computational Mathematics at KTH Royal Institute of Technology.

The projects explore the intersection of probabilistic modeling, stochastic calculus, and machine learning, bridging deep theoretical analysis with computational problem-solving.

---

## Repository Structure

### 📁 [Project 1: Stochastic Calculus Proofs](./Project_1_Stochastic_Calculus_Proofs)
A purely theoretical exploration of stochastic calculus, featuring rigorous mathematical proofs regarding Itô integrals and the $L^2$ convergence of stochastic discretizations.

### 📁 [Project 2: Numerical Solutions for SDEs](./Project_2_Numerical_SDEs)
Focuses on the Forward Euler approximation for SDEs. Features MATLAB implementations comparing exact analytical solutions against numerical reference approximations, evaluating both strong and weak convergence rates.

### 📁 [Project 3: Monte Carlo & Stochastic Volatility](./Project_3_Monte_Carlo_Volatility)
Explores Monte Carlo simulations for European call option pricing. Includes MATLAB implementations for analyzing sample variance and extending the models to incorporate stochastic volatility.

### 📁 [Project 4: Gibbs Sampling & Parameter Estimation](./Project_4_Gibbs_Sampling)
An exploration of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods. Compares frequentist and Bayesian parameter estimation and features a MATLAB implementation of a Gibbs Sampler to approximate joint distributions.

### 📁 [Project 5: Machine Learning & Pixel Variance](./Project_5_Machine_Learning)
Investigates anisotropic frequency distributions and pixel variance heatmaps for MNIST image classification, aiming to optimize algorithms by focusing on highly informative regions of the data.
